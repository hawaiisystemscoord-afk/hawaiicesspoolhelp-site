Cesspool Conversion in Kohala, Hawaiʻi Island

This page is intended for readers in Kapaʻau, Hāwī, Waimea, Kawaihae, Puakō, Waikoloa, and Halaʻula. Community name alone does not establish a parcel's wastewater system or site conditions.

Act 125 (2017) requires cesspools in Hawaiʻi to be replaced by 2050, subject to the law and current Hawaiʻi Department of Health requirements. For a Kohala property, wastewater-system requirements and feasible options depend on parcel-specific evaluation, design, current agency requirements, and approvals.

Site evaluation and design factors

An Individual Wastewater System site evaluation considers site-specific factors such as soil, slope, limiting layers, and the area suitable for a system; a community name does not determine those conditions.

Slope is evaluated for an Individual Wastewater System, and allowable disposal designs have system-specific slope limits.

Rocky ground: Under Hawaiʻi Administrative Rules §11-62-33.1(a)(9), rock or other protrusions encountered beneath a septic tank require additional excavation and granular backfill as specified by the rule.

Limited space: A site evaluation considers whether sufficient usable and suitable land area is available for the proposed Individual Wastewater System. Proposed systems must meet the applicable minimum distances in Hawaiʻi Administrative Rules chapter 11-62, Table II, unless the director approves otherwise.

Sewer information

Sewer availability and connection eligibility must be confirmed for the specific parcel through current County of Hawaiʻi wastewater information or a direct utility determination.

Costs and timelines

No verified Kohala-wide conversion-cost range is provided here; actual cost depends on parcel conditions, approved design, contractor scope, current agency requirements, and other project-specific factors.

Process overview

The applicable steps can vary with the parcel, proposed system, project scope, current agency requirements, and approvals. Review the step-by-step cesspool conversion guide for a general overview before the property-specific evaluation begins.

Site evaluation. For an Individual Wastewater System under Hawaiʻi Administrative Rules chapter 11-62, the engineer performs a site evaluation that addresses the factors required by the rules. Site-evaluation findings inform the design, and plans must be revised when construction conditions differ as provided by the rules.

Design and permitting. The applicable approval path may require engineer-prepared or engineer-evaluated materials and approval by the director; requirements depend on the proposed system and project pathway.

Installation. Construction must follow the approved plans and applicable standards; contractor responsibilities and restoration scope depend on the approved project and contract.

Final inspection. In the authorization path described by Hawaiʻi Administrative Rules §11-62-31.1(f), an engineer's final inspection report certifies construction against the approved plans and records accepted changes and as-built information. Use is authorized in writing by the director after the applicable requirements are met; do not assume a universal agency-signoff or cesspool-closure sequence.
