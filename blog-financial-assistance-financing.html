Current Financial Assistance and Financing Information

By Hawaiʻi Cesspool Help · Published July 23, 2026 · 7 min read

Direct answer: What current financial assistance or financing may help a Hawaiʻi homeowner pay for cesspool conversion, and where should the latest program details be verified?

Current status as of July 23, 2026

Hawaiʻi DOH’s official financial-support page lists a 2026 Cesspool Conversion Revolving Loan Program. The authorizing law provides for low-interest or forgivable loans for eligible households to replace or convert cesspools to DOH-approved wastewater systems or connect to sewer.

The same DOH page says the program is under development and will be administered by the Hawaiʻi Green Infrastructure Authority. That means the law and program framework exist, but a homeowner should not assume that applications, final eligibility rules, loan terms, or funding are already available.

Where to verify the latest details

  • Hawaiʻi DOH Financial Support Programs for Cesspool Conversion for the official statewide status and links.
  • Hawaiʻi Green Infrastructure Authority for program-development updates, application information, and contact details as they become available.

Check both sources immediately before making a budget or signing a construction contract. Save a dated copy of the program guidance you relied on.

Do not confuse completed programs with open assistance

DOH lists the 2023 Hawaiʻi Cesspool Pilot Grant Program and the 2016–2020 qualified cesspool income-tax credit under “Completed Programs.” The pilot grant offered qualifying recipients reimbursement up to $20,000, but that historical amount does not establish a current grant entitlement.

Old news articles, application forms, social-media posts, or contractor statements can remain online after a program closes. Verify that an application window is currently open.

Information to prepare for future applications

  • Property address, TMK, ownership, and household information.
  • Evidence of the existing cesspool and any priority or risk information requested by the program.
  • Engineer evaluation, approved design, project scope, and cost documentation if required.
  • Income or eligibility records specified by the final program rules.
  • Contractor estimates, sewer-connection information, and proof of required licenses.
  • A clear understanding of whether assistance is a loan, forgivable loan, reimbursement, grant, tax benefit, or another structure.

Questions to ask before relying on financing

  • Is the program accepting applications today?
  • Who is eligible, and how is household income measured?
  • Does the program cover engineering, permits, construction, sewer connection, cesspool closure, or only certain costs?
  • Must work wait for approval, or can prior costs qualify?
  • Is assistance paid in advance, at milestones, or as reimbursement?
  • What interest, forgiveness, lien, term, insurance, or owner-occupancy conditions apply?
  • What happens if bids exceed the approved amount?

Other financing conversations

A homeowner may also ask banks, credit unions, mortgage professionals, or housing counselors about ordinary financing products. Those products are not DOH cesspool programs and may involve interest, fees, collateral, underwriting, or property-condition requirements.

Obtain a property-specific engineering scope before borrowing whenever possible. Financing a guessed system or incomplete scope can leave a gap between available funds and the approved project.

Time-sensitive disclaimer

As-of date: July 23, 2026. Program rules can change after publication. Hawaiʻi Cesspool Help does not administer public funding, determine eligibility, lend money, or guarantee approval. Use the official DOH and Hawaiʻi Green Infrastructure Authority pages for current instructions.
