Cesspool Conversion in Puna, Hawaiʻi Island

If you are planning a cesspool replacement for a property in Puna District, start with records for the specific parcel. Site conditions, available infrastructure, design options, permitting, cost, schedule, and maintenance requirements must be evaluated for that property rather than predicted from the district name.

The Hawaiʻi Department of Health (DOH) Wastewater Branch states that Act 125 requires all cesspools in Hawaiʻi to be replaced by 2050. DOH also describes four statewide priority categories. Those categories do not, by themselves, establish a particular parcel's priority or replacement design.

Confirm the property and available records

Before requesting a property-specific review, gather what is available for the parcel. The guide to checking your cesspool priority and TMK explains how to organize the parcel identifier and verify current information with official sources.

  • the property address and parcel identifier;
  • existing wastewater-system records, plans, permits, inspection notes, and maintenance records;
  • any site plan or survey you already have;
  • information or records you have about the property's water source, wells, and sewer service; and
  • the owner's intended project, timing, and any planned sale, refinance, or construction.

Do not infer district boundaries, sewer availability, groundwater conditions, setbacks, soil suitability, or a suitable replacement system from a community name or mailing address. Confirm those matters from current official records and parcel-specific professional review.

Questions for a property-specific review

Ask the professional reviewing the property:

  • Which parcel records and current site information are still needed?
  • What site investigation is needed before design options can be evaluated?
  • What wastewater options should be evaluated for this parcel, and what information supports that evaluation?
  • Which current rules, setbacks, approvals, and permits apply to the proposed work?
  • Does an official record confirm whether sewer service is available to this parcel?
  • What project-specific factors could affect cost, schedule, construction access, and maintenance?
  • What written design, permit, installation, operation, and maintenance records should the owner retain?

Official resources

  • Hawaiʻi DOH Wastewater Branch — Cesspools in Hawaiʻi: official information about cesspools, Act 125, and the four priority categories.
  • Hawaiʻi DOH report to the Legislature on cesspool replacement prioritization: the official prioritization report linked by DOH. This page does not use it to assign a parcel result.
  • Hawaiʻi DOH Wastewater Branch: official starting point for current wastewater program information.

Selling, refinancing, or planning other work

If a sale, refinance, or other property project is planned, ask the relevant licensed and transaction professionals what current parcel records and wastewater information are needed. This page does not predict how a buyer, lender, inspector, agency, or other party will treat a particular property or transaction.
